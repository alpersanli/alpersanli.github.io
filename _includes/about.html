Modulation Transfer Function (MTF) and Point Spread Function (PSF) are fundamental metrics for evaluating optical performance. MTF describes how contrast is preserved across spatial frequencies, while PSF shows the distribution of light from a point source. In telescope payload design, optimizing MTF and PSF directly improves image sharpness and mission success in Earth observation.

RTL-SDR.com provides low-cost software-defined radio dongles, enabling reception of a wide frequency range. With open-source software, they can be used for weather satellite image decoding, aircraft tracking (ADS-B), and even amateur radio astronomy experiments. A great entry point for exploring the electromagnetic spectrum.

CubeSat deployers such as P-PODs and newer deployer mechanisms are critical for small satellite missions. They ensure safe deployment from launch vehicles while protecting payloads. Innovations include spring-loaded systems, sequenced release, and modular multi-satellite deployment. Their design directly influences mission safety and orbital insertion accuracy.
